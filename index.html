Currently Rising

-My Journey-

First and foremost, Thank you so much for taking the time to read about me and allowing me to tell you a little bit more about who I am as a person. I am currently a junior Texas A&M University student studying computer science and mathematics so Gig' em! I will finish my bachelor of science in computer science and mathematics minor in December of 2023 and depending on my work and desire to learn it could very well be May of 2024. As of now, I have a standing GPA of 3.7/4.0 and some of my favorite classes that I have taken would be computer systems, a class allowing me to learn more about the operating systems and low-level coding in general, Programming Studios, a class revolving around group coding in which amazing products can be brought to life, and surprisingly enough, philosophy, a class with a lot of writing, but it allowed me to freely write my curious expressions down. That class had me leaving the class with more questions and a more curious behavior where I found myself putting "how" and "why" at the end of my thoughts. I am extremely interested in software development having experience with both front-end and back-end. This includes knowledge of Html5, JavaScript, and a bit of React as well as Java, Python, C++, SQL, and VBA. I am also interested in lower-level programming with an emphasis on Linux shells, multithreading, and client-server-side programming. When it comes to version control, I am proficient in git and GitHub desktop. However, aside from all of the skills I am advertising above, I think my most proud one has to be my willingness to learn more. I recognize there is still a vast multitude of things I can still learn, therefore I am still very curious and eager to learn more about what this field can offer. I Would like to describe myself as a hard worker, problem solver, and someone who loves communicating and creating meaningful relationships.

From here on out I would like to tell you all about my journey. It all started on a lovely summer of 2002 when there was a baby not looking the greatest after being pushed out prematurely 5 weeks early. Starting rough right off the bat, extremely little Michael Tao managed to push through and start his life. Fun fact, he was so small that when he was being x-rayed they thought he was going to be a dwarf, but now he is a healthy 5'10 (5'11 with shoes on)! I lived in Katy all my life with my parents and grandparents and My family means the entire world to me. I was bestowed with a fantastic childhood and I was raised with tender love, effective communication, new experiences, and 4 role models that I still look up to today. That is where I learned the importance of having empathy, being a lifelong learner, being a good listener, and being responsible and kind. I want to continue growing my character allowing the world to see that my parents did raise me well, and not only that, I want to continue striving toward greater ambitions so I can look back and say "Thank you, mom and dad, I have made it thanks to you, so now let me take care of you." As I continued growing, I took up many hobbies, and some that I love to partake in nowadays would be Swimming, basketball, weightlifting, concerts that let you experience music like never before, cross-stitching, and even looking to start making my own clothing! A part of me loves going out and doing things, but there's nothing like creating a tranquil atmosphere by playing cards with my grandparents, sitting around and having a good conversation with laughs, and being by yourself participating in self-love. Also, one of my biggest dreams is to travel around the world and engage with breathtaking scenery that is not just my suburbs in katy.

Now onto high school. I went to a rather large school called Obra D. Tompkins Highschool and while there were a lot of stressful and tiring moments, it was overshadowed by unforgettable memories, unbreakable bonds, and incredible life lessons. I remember that I used to be really shy around my peers, but if there is one thing I overcame throughout high school was my fear of stepping outside of my comfort zone. Some of the most exciting times of my life happened to be spontaneous and enhanced by the friends I made along the way throughout the years. Whether that be a trip to Galveston, random dinner runs after long grueling swim practice, or just even 3 am spontaneous I-Hop feasts. It may be cheesy but I want to thank every single one of my friends and mentors, it meant a lot. To me, one of the most important things I learned throughout high school was the word "Sonder." It quite literally means: the realization that each random passerby is living a life as vivid and complex as your own, in which you might appear only once, as an extra sipping coffee in the background. Imagine you are driving home from the airport and you see a giant apartment complex building with many windows lit up. It is hard to believe, but every one of those windows has someone living their own story. Whether it may be a big job interview, a tired doctor, or even a couple celebrating their honeymoon, they all have something going on. This word means so much to me because every single person has spent years living on this planet and has a story to tell. It helped me become a better listener, understand that everyone has something going on for them whether good or bad and allow me to stretch my hand out to introduce myself instead of shyly sitting next to someone for the entirety of a semester without getting to know that person. I believe that nobody is just boring, sometimes you just need someone to listen. I want to take this time to tell every single person to take that initiative and step outside of their comfort zone, for you never know what relationship could blossom. It is always the most unlikely friendship that flips your world upside down for the better. This next section revolves around that unlikely friendship I made in high school where I was able to learn so much. That person continues to make me challenge myself and motivates me to be better, and not only that, I was able to learn the true meaning of fulfillment and what it means to pursue happiness. Never have I been able to smile continuously and laugh to the point where it feels like time has stopped and you are just in my own little bubble. Thanks for being my best friend. All in all, I just want to say, I am not who I am today by myself. I have an amazing group of friends and family that continuously allow me to strive toward my ambitions. I wouldn't be the same person if it wasn't for them and I don't think I would like that person either. I am continuing my journey at Texas A&M and working hard toward a life I can look back at and say I have made it. It may have been a little bit overbearing and potentially cringy, but thank you for reading and learning a little more about what goes on in my mind! - Michael Tao
